📘 OSI Model – Layer 1: Physical Layer

The **Physical Layer** is the **lowest level (Layer 1)** of the OSI model.  
It deals with the **physical connection** between devices and **transmits raw bits** (0s and 1s) over communication channels.

──────────────────────────────────────────
✅ Responsibilities:
──────────────────────────────────────────

1. 🧲 Bit Transmission  
   - Sends raw bits (0s and 1s) via electrical/light/radio signals.

2. 🔌 Physical Medium Definition  
   - Defines cables, connectors, ports (e.g., RJ45, fiber, coaxial).

3. 🎚️ Data Rate  
   - Specifies transmission speed (e.g., 100 Mbps, 1 Gbps).

4. 🎛️ Signal Encoding  
   - Defines how 0s and 1s are represented (e.g., voltages, pulses).

5. ⌛ Synchronization  
   - Syncs sender and receiver to read bits correctly.

6. ❌ No Data Meaning  
   - Doesn’t understand or modify data — just transmits bits.

──────────────────────────────────────────
🛠️ Devices That Work at Physical Layer:
──────────────────────────────────────────

- Cables (Twisted Pair, Coaxial, Fiber)
- Hubs
- Repeaters
- NICs (physical part)
- Modems

──────────────────────────────────────────
🧪 Examples of Physical Layer Technologies:
──────────────────────────────────────────

- Ethernet (cable & connectors)
- Wi-Fi (radio signals)
- Bluetooth
- USB
- DSL / Fiber optics

──────────────────────────────────────────
📦 Data Unit: Bits
──────────────────────────────────────────

- Transmits raw binary data
- No headers or structure

──────────────────────────────────────────
🧠 Real-Life Analogy:
──────────────────────────────────────────

The **postal vehicle** that physically carries your mail.
It doesn't care what’s inside the envelope, just delivers it.
