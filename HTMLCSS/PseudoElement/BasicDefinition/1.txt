Step 1: What is a CSS Pseudo-element?
A pseudo-element is a special keyword you add to a selector in CSS to style part of an 
element’s content, rather than the entire element itself.

Key points:
Pseudo-elements let you target sub-parts of an element (like just the first letter of a paragraph, 
or content before/after the element’s real content).

They’re written with two colons (::) followed by their name.
You don’t have to change your HTML to use them—they only exist in CSS and in the rendered page.
